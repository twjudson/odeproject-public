EquationÂ (5.5.3) tells us that \(dH/dt \leq 0\text{.}\) Moreover, \(dH/dt \lt 0\) whenever the distance between the two masses is changing. Thus, energy decreases whenever the second mass is moving relative to the first. Thus, if the wind or an earthquake starts our building (\(m_1\)) swaying back and forth, then the tuned mass-damper (\(m_2\)) located on one of the top floors of the building will start to move relative to the building and energy will be removed from the system by the dampers.

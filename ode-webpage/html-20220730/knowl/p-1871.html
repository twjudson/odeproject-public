If \(\alpha \lt 0\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} J = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \sqrt{-\alpha} & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \text{ or } \begin{pmatrix} -2 \sqrt{-\alpha} & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}

In the first case, we have a saddle since the eigenvalues are real and of opposite sign. In the second case, we have a nodal sink, since both eigenvalues are negative. The two equilibrium solutions move in opposite directions as \(\alpha\) decreases.

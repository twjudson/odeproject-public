Consider the system

\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} x'(t) \\ y'(t) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -5 \amp -2 \\ -1 \amp -4 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x(t) \\ y(t) \end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}

The eigenvalues of this system are \(\lambda_1 = -6\) and \(\lambda_2 = -3\) with eigenvectors \(\mathbf v_1 = (2,1 )\) and \(\mathbf v_2 = (1, -1)\text{,}\) respectively. Since the dominant eigenvalue is \(\lambda_1 = -6\text{,}\) solutions tend towards the straight-line solution containing the vector \(\mathbf v_1 = (2,1 )\) more quickly (FigureÂ 3.3.8).

One the other hand, suppose that \(T \lt 0\text{.}\) Then the eigenvalue \((T - \sqrt{T^2 - 4D}\,)/2\) is always negative, and we need to determine if other eigenvalue is positive or negative. If \(D \lt 0\text{,}\) then \(T^2 - 4D \gt T^2\) and \(\sqrt{T^2 - 4D} \gt T\text{.}\) Therefore, the other eigenvalue \((T - \sqrt{T^2 - 4D}\,)/2\) is positive, telling us that any point in the fourth quadrant must correspond to a saddle. If \(D \gt 0\text{,}\) then \(\sqrt{T^2 - 4D} \lt T\) and the second eigenvalue is negative. In this case, we will have a nodal sink. We summarize our findings in FigureÂ 3.7.6.

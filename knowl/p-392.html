Consider the initial value problem
\begin{gather*} \frac{dy}{dx} - \frac{2}{x} y = 2x^2\\ y(-2) = 4. \end{gather*}
Our integrating factor is
\begin{equation*} \mu(x) = \exp\left( - \int \frac{2}{x} \, dx\right) = e^{-2 \ln x} = e^{\ln x^{-2}} = x^{-2}. \end{equation*}
Multiplying both of our differential equation by \(\mu(x) = x^{-2}\text{,}\) we obtain
\begin{equation*} x^{-2} \left(\frac{dy}{dx} - \frac{2}{x} y \right) = 2 \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}\left( x^{-2} y \right) = x^{-2} \frac{dy}{dx} - 2 x^{-3} y = 2. \end{equation*}
We can now integrate this equation to get
\begin{equation*} x^{-2} y = 2x + C. \end{equation*}
The initial condition \(y(-2) = 4\) allows us to find \(C = 5\text{.}\) Therefore, the solution to our initial value problem is
\begin{equation*} y = 2x^3 + 5x^2. \end{equation*}

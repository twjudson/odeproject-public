Making the substitution \(u = x - 1\) and \(v = y - 1\text{,}\) simply translates the entire system to the origin, and we obtain the system
\begin{align} \frac{du}{dt} \amp = u(u - 2) - v\tag{5.1.13}\\ \frac{dv}{dt} \amp u(u^2 + 3u + 3) - v.\tag{5.1.14} \end{align}
Our new system has \(u\) and \(v\)-nullclines \(v = u(u - 2)\) and \(v = u(u^2 + 3u + 3)\text{,}\) respectively. Notice that we have simply moved the phase portrait of the original system so that our equilibrium solution is now at the origin. Furthermore, we can approximate the \(u\) and \(v\)-nullclines by their tangent lines \(v = -2u\) and \(v = 3u\text{,}\) respectively.

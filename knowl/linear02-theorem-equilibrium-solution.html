Theorem 3.2.2.

Let
\begin{equation*} \frac{d {\mathbf x}}{dt} = A {\mathbf x} \end{equation*}
be a \(2 \times 2\) linear system, where \(A\) is not the zero matrix.
  1. If \(\det(A) \neq 0\text{,}\) then \((x, y) = (0, 0)\) is the unique equilibrium solution for the system.
  2. If \(\det(A) = 0\text{,}\) then the equilibrium solutions for the system form a straight line in \({\mathbb R}^2\text{.}\)

Objective.

To understand that the characteristic polynomial of a \(2 \times 2\) matrix can be written as
\begin{equation*} \lambda^2 - T \lambda + D, \end{equation*}
where \(T = \trace(A)\) and \(D = \det(A)\text{.}\) Furthermore, if a \(2 \times 2\) matrix \(A\) has eigenvalues \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\text{,}\) then \(\trace(A)\) is \(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2\) and \(\det(A) = \lambda_1 \lambda_2\text{,}\) and the trace and determinant of a \(2 \times 2\) matrix are invariant under a change of coordinates.

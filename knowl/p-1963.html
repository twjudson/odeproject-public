Suppose that \(S(x, y)\) is defined on the rectangle \(-3 \leq x \leq 3\) and \(-3 \leq y \leq 3\) by
\begin{equation*} S(x,y) = \frac{y^2}{2} - \frac{y^4}{4} - \frac{x^2}{2} + 6. \end{equation*}
  1. What is the gradient system for \(S\text{?}\)
  2. Show that
    \begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dt} S(x(t), y(t)) = \frac{\partial S}{\partial x} \frac{dx}{dt} + \frac{\partial S}{\partial y} \frac{dy}{dt} \geq 0. \end{equation*}
    Hence, we can deduce that \(S\) is increasing whenever \(\nabla S \neq 0\text{.}\)
  3. Sketch the level sets of \(S\) for \(-3 \leq x \leq 3\) and \(-3 \leq y\ leq 3\text{.}\)
  4. Sketch the vector field given by
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = \frac{\partial S}{\partial x}\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = \frac{\partial S}{\partial y}. \end{align*}
    What do you notice about the two plots?
  5. If
    \begin{equation*} S(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 - \frac{x^4 + y^4}{4} - 5x^2y^2 + 50, \end{equation*}
    how many dead fish will there be? Where are the dead fish located?

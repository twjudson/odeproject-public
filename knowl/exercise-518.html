Exercise 4.

Consider the system of differential equations
\begin{align*} x' & = x(-x -y + 1)\\ y' & = y(-ax -y + b), \end{align*}
where \(a\) and \(b\) are parameters with \(a, b \gt 0\text{.}\) Suppose that this system is only defined for \(x, y \geq 0\text{.}\)
  1. Use nullclines to sketch the phase portrait for this system for several values of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\)
  2. Determine the values of \(a\) and \(b\) at which a bifurcation occurs.

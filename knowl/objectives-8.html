Objectives 2.1.

  • To understand and be able to create models using system of differential equations
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = f(x, y)\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = g(x, y). \end{align*}
  • To understand that solutions to the system
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = f(x, y),\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = g(x, y), \end{align*}
    as parametric plots in the \(xy\)-plane.
  • To understand that a second-order linear equation
    \begin{equation*} a(t) x'' + b(t) x' + c(t) x = g(t) \end{equation*}
    can be written as a system of first-order equations by letting \(v(t) = x'(t)\text{.}\)
  • To understand that equilibrium solutions for a system of differential equations
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = f(x, y)\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = g(x, y) \end{align*}
    are those values of \(x\) and \(y\) such that both \(f(x, y) = 0\) and \(g(x, y) = 0\text{.}\)

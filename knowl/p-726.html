Suppose that \(x = x(t)\) and \(y = y(t)\) are two populations, competing for the same resources, are governed by the following system of differential equations,
\begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = x(1-x) - \alpha xy\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = y(1-y) - \alpha xy. \end{align*}
If we study how the two populations interact, we will discover two very different cases depending on the value of the parameter \(\alpha\text{.}\)

Bernoulli's equation is an equation of the form
\begin{equation*} y' = a(t) y + f(t) y^n, \end{equation*}
where \(n \neq 0\) or 1. Bernoulli's equation is nonlinear and cannot be solved by the techniques that we have used to solve first order linear equations. 2 
  1. Using the substitution \(z = y^{1- n}\text{,}\) show that we can transform Bernoulli's equation into the linear equation
    \begin{equation*} z' = (1 -n )a(t) z + (1-n) f(t). \end{equation*}
  2. Solve the equation \(xy' + y = x^4y^3\text{.}\)

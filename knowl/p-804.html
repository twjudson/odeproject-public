The surprising thing about Duffing's equation is that it is extremely sensitive to initial conditions. A slight change in the initial conditions can yield dramatically different solutions. If we change the initial velocity to \(v(0) = 1.7653\text{,}\) we obtain a very different graph of the solution (FigureÂ 2.3.5 ). For small initial velocities, solutions spiral towards the origin. However, a larger initial velocity will send the solution in the phase plane away from the origin. If the crane sways too violently, we will have a disaster.

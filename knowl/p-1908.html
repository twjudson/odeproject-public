Recall that the gradient of a function \(V: {\mathbb R}^2 \rightarrow {\mathbb R}\) is
\begin{equation*} \nabla V = \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial V}{\partial y}\right). \end{equation*}
If
\begin{equation*} {\mathbf F}(x, y) = \begin{pmatrix} f(x, y) \\ g(x, y) \end{pmatrix}, \end{equation*}
then
\begin{equation*} \dot{V} = \nabla V \cdot {\mathbf F}, \end{equation*}
is the directional derivative of \(V\) in the direction of \({\mathbf F}\text{.}\)

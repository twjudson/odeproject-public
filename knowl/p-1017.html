We may also have matrices such as
\begin{equation*} C = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
has complex eigenvalues, \(\lambda = 4 - i\) and \(\mu = 4 + i\text{.}\) Eigenvectors for \(\lambda\) and \(\mu\) are \(\mathbf u = (1, -i)\) and \(\mathbf v = (1, i)\text{,}\) respectively.

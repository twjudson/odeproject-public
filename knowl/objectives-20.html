Objectives 3.9.

  • To understand that \({\mathbf x}(t) = e^{tA}{\mathbf x}_0\) is the solution to the initial value problem
    \begin{align*} {\mathbf x}' & = A {\mathbf x}\\ {\mathbf x}(0) & = {\mathbf x}_0, \end{align*}
    where \(e^{tA}\) is the matrix exponential.
  • To understand that if \(\lambda\) be an eigenvalue of an \(n \times n\) matrix A and \({\mathbf v}\) is an eigenvector for \(\lambda\text{,}\) then \(e^{tA} {\mathbf v} = e^{\lambda t} {\mathbf v}\text{.}\)
  • To understand and be able to apply the properties of the matrix exponential to \(n \times n\) matrices \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\)
  • To understand and be able to use the matrix exponential to solve a linear system
    \begin{align*} {\mathbf x}' & = A {\mathbf x}\\ {\mathbf x}(0) & = {\mathbf x}_0. \end{align*}

The eigenvalues of \(A\) are given by
\begin{equation*} \lambda = \frac{T \pm \sqrt{T^2 - 4D}}{2}. \end{equation*}
If \(T^2 - 4D \lt 0\text{,}\) then we have a complex eigenvalues, and the type of equilibrium point depends on the real part of the eigenvalue. The sign of the real part is determined solely by \(T\text{.}\) If \(T \gt 0\) we have a source. If \(T \lt 0\text{,}\) we have a sink. If \(T = 0\text{,}\) we have a center. See FigureÂ 3.7.5.

Exercise 7.

Consider the system
\begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = -x + y^2\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = 3x^2 - 2y. \end{align*}
  1. Find the equilibrium solutions of the system. Which one of the equilibrium solutions is stable?
  2. Verify that the function
    \begin{equation*} V(x, y) = \frac{x^2}{2} + \frac{y^2}{4} \end{equation*}
    is a Lyapunov function for the system, and calculate \(\dot{V}(x,y)\text{.}\)
  3. Sketch the level sets of \(V\text{.}\)
  4. Sketch the phase portrait of the system.

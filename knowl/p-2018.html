Now let us consider a different approach. If we let
\begin{align*} p_1 & = m_1 x'_1\\ p_2 & = m_2 x'_2, \end{align*}
show that the system (5.5.1)–(5.5.2) can be written as a system of four first-order linear equations.
\begin{align*} x'_1 & = \frac{p_1}{m_1}\\ x'_2 & = \frac{p_2}{m_2}\\ p'_1 & = -k_1 x_1 - b_1\frac{p_1}{m_1} - k_2(x_1 - x_2) - b_2\left( \frac{p_1}{m_1} - \frac{p_2}{m_2} \right) + \rho_0 \cos(\omega t)\\ p'_2 & = -k_2(x_2 - x_1) - b_2\left( \frac{p_2}{m_2} - \frac{p_1}{m_1} \right). \end{align*}
In the case of no damping, this is
\begin{align*} x'_1 & = \frac{p_1}{m_1}\\ x'_2 & = \frac{p_2}{m_2}\\ p'_1 & = -k_1 x_1 - k_2(x_1 - x_2) + \rho_0 \cos(\omega t)\\ p'_2 & = -k_2(x_2 - x_1). \end{align*}
Of course, you have already derived these systems in Task 5.5.2.a and Task 5.5.2.b.

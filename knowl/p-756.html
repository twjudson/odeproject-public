  • The righthand side of the system
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = f(x, y)\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = g(x, y) \end{align*}
    can be viewed as a vector field, \((f(x,y), g(x,y))\text{,}\) which can be plotted in the \(x,y\)-plane.
  • Competition between two competing species can be modeled with the system
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = \alpha x \left(1 - \frac{x}{M} \right) - \beta xy\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = \gamma y \left(1 - \frac{y}{N} \right) - \delta xy. \end{align*}
  • We can use nullclines and phase plane analysis to sketch solution curves for the system
    \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} & = f(x, y)\\ \frac{dy}{dt} & = g(x, y). \end{align*}
